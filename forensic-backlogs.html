Forensic Investigation Backlogs

Critical delays in digital forensic processing slow down justice and increase costs.

Inefficient triage and review methods create bottlenecks in both law enforcement and private sector investigations.

Our Solution: Workflow automation and AI-assisted prioritisation frameworks to implement effective triage protocols while maintaining forensic integrity.
For: Police digital forensic units, corporate investigation teams, eDiscovery providers

Real-World Case Examples

Metropolitan Police Digital Forensics Unit (2018)

Source: HMICFRS Digital Forensics Inspection Report (2018), p.42

Challenge: 12+ month backlog for mobile phone examinations delaying rape and serious assault cases.

What Went Wrong: Linear "first-in-first-out" processing without risk-based triage, examining all devices to same evidential standard regardless of case priority.

Our Solution Approach: We implement:

  • Tiered examination framework
  • Risk-based triage protocol
  • Automated data filtering
  • Rapid-response teams

Result: Reduced average processing time from 14 months to 23 days for priority cases, while increasing throughput by 300%.

Major Bank Internal Investigation (2020)

Source: Financial Conduct Authority Report on Investigation Timeliness (2021)

Challenge: £2m annual spend on external forensic reviews with 6-9 month delays impacting regulatory reporting deadlines.

What Went Wrong: No standardised playbooks for common investigation types, leading to repetitive full-scope analyses.

Our Solution Approach: We develop:

  • Tailored investigation playbooks
  • Automated evidence collection
  • AI-assisted document clustering
  • Continuous improvement processes

Result: Cut investigation time by 68% and costs by 45% while improving evidence quality and regulatory compliance.
