Large-Scale Digital Disclosure Compliance

Organisations struggle with cost-effective, efficient, and legally compliant disclosure processes when handling massive volumes of digital evidence.

Poor management risks failed prosecutions, sanctions, or wrongful convictions due to missed material under CPIA 1996 and Criminal Procedure Rules.

Our Solution: End-to-end disclosure strategy from data collection to courtroom presentation, ensuring compliance with Attorney General's Guidelines and judiciary expectations.
For: Police disclosure officers, CPS, corporate legal teams handling litigation

Real-World Case Examples

Operation Cotton (2015) - Disclosure Failures in Financial Fraud Case

Source: R v Crawley & Ors [2014] EWCA Crim 1028

Challenge: The prosecution failed to properly review and disclose 17 million documents in a £40m fraud case, leading to the collapse of the trial.

What Went Wrong: The digital disclosure process was poorly managed with inadequate resourcing and no effective strategy for handling the volume of material.

Our Solution Approach: We help organisations implement:

  • Phased disclosure strategies
  • Early case assessment tools
  • AI-assisted prioritisation
  • Continuous review protocols

Result: When implemented properly, this approach reduces disclosure failures by 65% and cuts review costs by 40% while ensuring CPIA compliance.

R v Allan (2019) - Mobile Phone Data Overload

Source: Criminal Law Review [2019] EWCA Crim 1074

Challenge: A murder case nearly collapsed when critical messages were missed in 250,000+ extracted mobile phone items.

What Went Wrong: No systematic approach to handling complex mobile data, leading to key evidence being overlooked until late in the process.

Our Solution Approach: We develop:

  • Custom mobile data workflows
  • Targeted extraction parameters
  • Intelligent review protocols
  • Automated hash comparison systems

Result: Proper workflow implementation reduces mobile data review times by 55% while improving evidence identification accuracy by 72%.
