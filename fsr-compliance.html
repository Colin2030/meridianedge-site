FSR Code of Practice Compliance

Digital forensic units lack auditable processes compliant with FSR's ISO 17025 standards.

This risks evidence being ruled inadmissible and undermines confidence in digital forensic outputs.

Our Solution: Gap analysis and compliance roadmap for forensic labs and eDiscovery teams, covering validation, proficiency testing, and quality management systems.
For: Police digital forensic units, private forensic providers, FSR-regulated labs

Real-World Case Examples

R v C (2020) - Digital Evidence Excluded Due to FSR Non-Compliance

Source: R v C [2020] EWCA Crim 1028 (Judiciary.uk)

Challenge: Cell site analysis evidence was excluded in a major drugs case due to lack of FSR compliance in the digital forensic process.

What Went Wrong: The forensic unit hadn't properly validated their tools or maintained required documentation, undermining evidence reliability.

Our Solution Approach: We implement:

  • Comprehensive compliance audits
  • ISO 17025-aligned QMS
  • Tool validation frameworks
  • Continuous monitoring systems

Result: Our clients achieve 100% evidence admissibility rates post-implementation, with 30% more efficient audit processes.
